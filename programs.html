The Sport of Goalball

Experience the most popular team sport designed specifically for the blind and visually impaired.

What is Goalball?

Goalball was created in 1946 to help rehabilitate veterans who had lost their sight during World War II. Since then, it has evolved into a high-speed, competitive Paralympic sport played in over 100 countries.

It's played by two teams of three players on a court the size of a volleyball court. The objective is to throw a ball that has bells inside into the opponent's goal, which spans the entire width of the court.

Key Rules & Mechanics

Eyeshades

To ensure total equality, all players must wear blackout eyeshades (masks) at all times, regardless of their level of visual impairment.

The Ball

The ball contains bells that allow players to track its movement using their hearing. The audience must remain completely silent during play.

The Court

The court features tactile lines (raised lines) that players use to orient themselves through touch.

Join Our Practices

We provide all the necessary equipment for beginners, including eyeshades, pads, and balls. You just need to bring athletic clothes and a passion for the game.
