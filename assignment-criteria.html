Assignment Criteria

Course Information

  • Course: Web Design & Development
  • Instructor: Professor Williams
  • Assignment Title: Comprehensive Organizational Website Project
  • Due Date: May 29, 2026 at 11:59 PM via Canvas
  • Submission Method: Canvas + OneDrive Link

Assignment Overview

For this major course project, you will design, develop, and publish a fully functional, multi-page website for an organization of your choice. The organization may be:

  • fictional,
  • currently operating but lacking a strong web presence, or
  • an existing organization whose website you will conceptually redesign (you may not copy their code or assets).

This assignment is intended to synthesize everything learned in this course, including HTML, CSS, layout principles, responsive design, accessibility, usability, visual hierarchy, and basic site architecture. Your final product should reflect professional-level thinking, intentional design choices, and clean, well-structured code.

In addition to the website, you will submit a written paper (1,500–1,700 words) explaining your design decisions, development process, accessibility considerations, and evaluation results.

Core Project Requirements

Your website must be comprehensive and demonstrate thoughtful planning and execution. At minimum, the site must include:

  • A homepage
  • At least three additional content pages
  • A consistent global navigation menu
  • A functional footer
  • Responsive design for mobile and desktop
  • Original or properly attributed media
  • Accessibility best practices implemented throughout

Detailed Assignment Criteria

1. Organizational Concept and Content Strategy

Your selected organization must have a clearly defined mission, audience, and purpose. Whether fictional or real, you must present the organization as if it were operating in the real world. The site content should reflect a deep understanding of who the organization serves and what users need when visiting the website.

2. Information Architecture and Navigation Design

Your website must demonstrate intentional and user-centered information architecture. The navigation menu should be clearly visible, logically organized, and consistent across all pages. Users should be able to understand the structure of your site within seconds of arriving.

3. Visual Design, Layout, and Color System

Your site must demonstrate strong visual hierarchy and thoughtful aesthetic choices. This is not simply about making the site “look good,” but about using design principles intentionally to guide the user’s attention.

4. Accessibility and Inclusive Design (High Priority)

Accessibility is a major grading component. Your site must demonstrate serious effort toward inclusive design. You are expected to apply principles discussed in class, including semantic HTML and assistive technology support.

5. Images, Media, and Visual Assets

All images and media must be high quality, appropriately sized, and legally usable. Images must enhance the user experience rather than serve as decoration without purpose.

6. Responsive and Mobile-First Design

Your website must function effectively across multiple screen sizes. Layouts should adapt smoothly without horizontal scrolling or broken elements. Navigation must remain usable on small screens.

7. Code Quality and Technical Structure

Your code should reflect professional practices learned in this course. This includes clean indentation, meaningful comments, separation of HTML and CSS, and semantic markup.

8. Required Footer Elements

Your website footer must appear on every page and include: copyright information, contact or organizational reference, a visible link to your Linktree, and a clearly labeled section outlining these assignment requirements.

9. External Feedback and Optional Publishing

If you would like formative feedback on your site, you are encouraged (but not required) to publish your website using a hosting provider.

10. Written Paper Requirements (1,500–1,700 words)

In addition to the website, you must submit a formal written analysis of your project. The paper must be between 1,500 and 1,700 words and written in an academic tone.

Submission Instructions

  1. Upload your entire website source code to OneDrive.
  2. Submit the shareable OneDrive link via Canvas.
  3. Submit your written paper via Canvas.
  4. If published, include your live site URL in the submission.
