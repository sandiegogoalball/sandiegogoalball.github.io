About Goalball

The premier team sport designed specifically for the blind and visually impaired.

What is Goalball?

Goalball is a Paralympic sport played by two teams of three players each. The objective is to throw a ball that has bells inside of it into the opponent's goal.

Players use their bodies to block the incoming ball, relying entirely on their sense of hearing and tactile cues from the court lines. Because all players must wear "blackout" eyeshades, the sport is perfectly inclusive for athletes with varying degrees of visual impairment.

Key Rules of the Game

01

The Quiet Rule

Spectators must remain completely silent during play so athletes can hear the ball. Cheering is only allowed after a goal or during official breaks.

02

10-Second Rule

Once a team gains control of the ball, they have only 10 seconds to throw it back at the opponent's goal.

03

Eyeshade Penalties

Touching your eyeshades without permission from the referee can result in a penalty, as it could be seen as an attempt to gain a visual advantage.
