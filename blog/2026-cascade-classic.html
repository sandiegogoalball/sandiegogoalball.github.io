SD Goalball Shines at Cascade Classic

23 Feb

Tournament Recap

By Lori Meyers

Last week, San Diego County Goalball traveled to Portland, Oregon to compete in the 16th Annual Cascade Classic. It was a weekend filled with intense competition, personal growth, and wonderful community building.

The Cascade Classic is one of the premier goalball tournaments on the West Coast, attracting teams from across the United States and Canada. For many of our newer athletes, this was their first major travel tournament, and they handled the pressure with incredible grace and determination.

Tournament Highlights

Our team played a total of five matches over three days. While the competition was fierce, our athletes showed remarkable defensive skills, particularly in our match against the Seattle Sonics, where we held a clean sheet for the first half of the game.

"I'm so proud of how everyone supported each other," said Lori Meyers. "Beyond the scores, seeing our veteran players mentor the newcomers on and off the court is what this sport is truly about."

"Goalball isn't just about the game; it's about the confidence and community we build together."

What's Next?

We're back in San Diego now and ready to resume our regular practice schedule. We'll be focusing on the tactical lessons we learned in Portland as we prepare for our next regional tournament.

If you're interested in joining us, whether as an athlete or a volunteer, please check out our practice schedule. We'd love to have you on the court!
