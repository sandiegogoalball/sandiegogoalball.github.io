Rules of Goalball

A unique sport defined by sound and silence.

The Objective

The primary objective of goalball is to throw a ball past the opposing team and into their goal. The opposing team must use their bodies to block the ball before it crosses the goal line.

Silence is Key

Unlike most sports where fans are encouraged to cheer, goalball requires absolute silence during play. Athletes rely on their hearing to track the ball's position. Spectators are only permitted to cheer after a goal is scored or during official breaks.

Quick Facts

  • ● Players: 3 per team on court
  • ● Game Time: Two 12-minute halves
  • ● Overtime: Two 3-minute halves (Golden Goal)
  • ● Mercy Rule: 10-goal difference

The Court & Equipment

The Court

The court measures 18 meters long by 9 meters wide. All lines are marked with tape over string, creating tactile boundaries that players can feel through their feet and hands.

The Goals

Goals span the entire 9-meter width of each end of the court. They are 1.3 meters high. Blocking a throw across such a wide area requires extreme coordination and agility.

Eyeshades

To ensure fair play regardless of an athlete's degree of vision, all players must wear "blackout" eyeshades. These are often further secured with eye patches underneath.

Common Infractions & Penalties

High Ball

The ball must hit the court before it crosses the "high ball line" (6 meters from the thrower's end). If it stays in the air too long, it's a penalty.

Long Ball

The ball must hit the court at least once in the neutral zone (middle section of the court). If it skips over the neutral zone, it's a long ball penalty.

10-Second Rule

A team has 10 seconds to throw the ball back after it makes contact with any part of a defending player.

Illegal Coaching

Coaches can only provide feedback during timeouts or official breaks. Coaching during live play results in a penalty.

Note: When a penalty occurs, the offending player must defend the entire 9-meter goal alone for one throw from the opposing team.
