Accessibility Statement

San Diego County Goalball is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all users, including individuals with disabilities. We continuously improve the user experience for everyone and apply relevant accessibility standards whenever possible.

Our Commitment

San Diego County Goalball is dedicated to providing a website that is accessible, inclusive, and usable for the widest possible audience. We believe access to information about adaptive sports should never be limited by technology or disability. Our organization actively works to identify and remove barriers that may prevent full digital participation.

Accessibility is an ongoing process. As technologies evolve and standards improve, we regularly review our website to enhance usability for individuals with visual, auditory, motor, cognitive, and neurological disabilities. Our goal is to create an equitable digital environment that supports independence and full participation.

Standards and Guidelines

Our accessibility efforts are guided by the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, developed by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). These guidelines define how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities and more user-friendly for everyone.

San Diego County Goalball currently aims to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA success criteria. While many areas of the site meet or exceed these standards, some content may not yet fully conform. We are actively working to address known issues and improve overall compliance.

Accessibility Features

To support an inclusive browsing experience, our website incorporates the following accessibility features:

  • Keyboard Navigation: All primary navigation and interactive components are operable using a keyboard alone.
  • Visible Focus Indicators: Clear focus states help keyboard and low-vision users track their position on the page.
  • Skip Navigation Link: Users can bypass repetitive navigation and jump directly to main content.
  • High-Contrast Display Options: Users can switch to enhanced contrast modes for improved readability.
  • Semantic HTML Structure: Proper heading hierarchy and landmark roles improve screen reader navigation.
  • ARIA Attributes: Dynamic components include ARIA labels and state announcements where appropriate.
  • Responsive Design: Content is accessible across mobile devices, tablets, and desktop environments.
  • Readable Typography: We use scalable fonts and adequate spacing to improve readability.

Assistive Technology Compatibility

Our website is designed to be compatible with commonly used assistive technologies, including screen readers, screen magnifiers, speech recognition software, and alternative input devices. We routinely test using tools such as NVDA, VoiceOver, Lighthouse, and AXE accessibility testing utilities.

Because assistive technologies and browsers evolve frequently, compatibility may vary depending on the specific combination of software versions and user configurations.

Ongoing Improvements

Accessibility is integrated into our design and development workflow. We conduct periodic audits, manual keyboard testing, and automated scans to identify opportunities for improvement. Planned enhancements include:

  • Expanded alternative text coverage for all media
  • Improved PDF and document accessibility
  • Additional user preference controls
  • Continued color contrast optimization
  • Enhanced form labeling and validation feedback

Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some areas of the website may not yet be fully accessible. For example:

  • Some third-party content may not fully conform to accessibility standards.
  • Older archived documents may not be optimized for screen readers.
  • Certain legacy pages are still undergoing remediation.

If you encounter any accessibility barriers, we encourage you to contact us so we can provide the information you need in an accessible format.
