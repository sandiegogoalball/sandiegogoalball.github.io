History of Goalball

From rehabilitation to the world stage.

The Origins (1946)

Goalball was invented in 1946 by Austrian Hanz Lorenzen and German Sepp Reindl. It was originally designed as a way to keep blind World War II veterans active and to assist in their physical and mental rehabilitation after returning from the front.

Unlike many other sports for the visually impaired, goalball was not adapted from an existing sport played by sighted individuals. It was created from the ground up specifically for athletes with visual impairments.

Global Recognition

The sport gained international attention throughout the 1950s and 60s. It was introduced as a demonstration sport at the 1972 Paralympic Games in Heidelberg, Germany. Due to its success and growing popularity, it became an official medal sport at the 1976 Paralympics in Toronto.

The women's event was added to the Paralympic program at the 1984 Games in New York. Today, goalball is played in over 100 countries and is governed by the International Blind Sports Federation (IBSA).

San Diego Goalball Roots

In San Diego, goalball has a long-standing tradition of community and competitive spirit. Our local program was established to provide a space where athletes could train year-round. Over the decades, San Diego has produced numerous national-level players and has been a hub for goalball in Southern California.

Leaders like Lori and Neal Meyers have been instrumental in keeping the program alive, securing gym space at locations like the Mission Valley YMCA and organizing travel to regional tournaments across the West Coast.
